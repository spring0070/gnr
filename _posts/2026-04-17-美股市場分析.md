Tag: Sports Media, Entertainment

### English Summary
- The company's share price saw a recent drop of approximately 7% to 8%, after having rallied about 40% over the past eight weeks, suggesting a sell-on-the-news sentiment.
- Although the first quarter results were slightly better than guidance, they remained below analyst estimates. The second-quarter guidance is also anticipated to be below estimates, possibly due to delays in implementing US price increases.
- Despite the stock dip, the company is viewed as a growth stock, expected to maintain top-line growth in the low double digits.
- The company maintains its full-year revenue and operating income estimates, providing a measure of comfort to investors.
- Margin pressures are attributed to increasing sports rights costs (e.g., NFL renegotiations, adding games on holidays) and the expensive nature of major entertainment programming in the fourth quarter.
- Analysts suggested that investors should view the current decline as a "timing issue" rather than a fundamental weakness, suggesting it could be an opportune time for investors to buy back in.
