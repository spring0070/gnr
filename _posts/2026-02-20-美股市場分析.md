### 中文摘要

- 美國與加拿大等西方國家原油出口量攀升，2023年每日淨液態燃料與原油出口達800萬桶，反映全球能源供應重心逐漸向西半球轉移。  
- 委內瑞拉因與古巴關係緊張而被排除在國際能源網絡外，加速了南美洲其他國家如巴西、阿根廷等地的能源供應角色。  
- 市場預期若伊朗行動未導致實際供應中斷，可能僅加速現有跌勢，並透過「短線拉漲洗盤」後再回落。  

Tag: 彭博、沙特阿拉伯、委內瑞拉、加拿大、巴西、阿根廷、蓋亞那

### English Summary

- Oil prices rise to $66.50, a 2% increase, driven by potential Iranian strike tensions and Middle East supply disruptions.  
- Analyst Mike McGlone notes a "bear market bounce," with prices near last year's average but below the 2022 high of $80.  
- Market focus on OPEC's capacity to offset supply shocks, particularly Saudi Arabia's ability to ramp up production.  
- Geopolitical risks, such as Strait of Hormuz blockage, may not significantly impact prices due to existing supply surpluses.  
- Shift in global oil supply toward the Western Hemisphere (US, Canada, Brazil, Argentina, Guyana) reduces reliance on Middle Eastern producers.  
- Venezuela's role in regional supply trends and its geopolitical ties to Cuba are highlighted as factors in market dynamics.  
- President Trump's policies to lower oil prices via Venezuela and increased US/Canada exports influence market sentiment.  
- Historical context suggests military conflicts may initially spike prices but fail to sustain long-term highs.  

Tag: [OPEC, Saudi Arabia, Venezuela, United States, Canada, Brazil, Argentina, Guyana]
