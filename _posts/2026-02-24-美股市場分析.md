### English Summary

- The Strait of Hormuz is a critical oil transportation route, handling ~20 million barrels per day (25% of global seaborne oil trade) and 20% of global oil consumption.  
- Closure risks severe market disruption due to limited alternative routes (Saudi Arabia’s pipeline: 5 million bpd; UAE’s: 1.8 million bpd).  
- Protracted closure could cause significant price spikes and geopolitical tensions, affecting exporters (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Iran) and importers (China, India).  
- Short-term blockage may trigger immediate price volatility, with risk premiums rising sharply but easing as the closure duration shortens.  
- Geopolitical interests and economic dependencies at the Strait deter prolonged disruptions.
