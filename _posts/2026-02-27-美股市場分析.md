# 1. 標題: Jack Dorsey's Block Plans to Cut Nearly Half of Workforce in AI Bet

 [![觀看影片](https://i.ytimg.com/vi/drrb0vbV6h0/sddefault.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=drrb0vbV6h0){:target='_blank'}

## 以下是文章的主要重點：

### 中文摘要

1. **公司動態**  
   - Block（原Square）執行長Jack Dorsey推動組織瘦身，裁減40%員工（從10,000人降至6,000人），強調透過AI工具提升效率，減少人力成本。  
   - 投資內部AI工具「Goose」，並重申公司戰略來自「強勢基礎」，包括2025年營收成長、貸款業務擴張及盈利改善。  
   - 過去持續進行輪替裁員（自2024年起），認為過往擴張過度，需透過技術進步優化組織結構。  

2. **市場趨勢**  
   - 企業競逐AI效率化，Block與Meta等大廠同步探討技術驅動的組織轉型，但執行力度與成效受市場質疑。  
   - 公眾市場壓力下，Block需公開透明化策略，與私有企業的靈活決策形成對比。  
   - 市場對Block未來發展（如併購）持謹慎態度，因近期財報波動與業務方向不確定性較高。  

Tag: Block（原Square）、PayPal、Stripe、Metta

### English Summary

- Block (formerly Square) is restructuring by cutting 40% of its workforce (from ~10,000 to ~6,000 employees) to improve efficiency through AI tools like Goose.  
- Jack Dorsey emphasizes leveraging AI to reduce reliance on large teams, positioning the company as a leader in adopting technology for operational efficiency.  
- Market trends highlight Block’s stock decline (from $281 to $55 since 2021) and investor uncertainty about its long-term vision, including Bitcoin initiatives and potential acquisitions.  
- Speculation about Block’s future, such as a potential acquisition (e.g., PayPal), remains unclear, with management framing layoffs as a proactive move to strengthen the company.  
- Competitors like Meta and Stripe are also reevaluating workforce sizes, reflecting broader industry shifts toward tech-driven efficiency.  
- Publicly traded companies face pressure to communicate restructuring efforts, unlike private firms that might make similar decisions without public disclosure.  

Tag: [Block, PayPal, Meta, Stripe]

Tag: 華納兄弟、派拉蒙、Netflix

### English Summary

- **Warner Bros.** has accepted Paramount's offer as a "superior proposal," giving Paramount the lead in negotiations.  
- **Netflix** has four days to respond to Paramount's deal, which could result in losing the acquisition race.  
- **Market speculation** suggests shareholders are divided between preferring Paramount's certainty (full company purchase) vs. Netflix's partial acquisition of divisions.  
- **Regulatory concerns** remain a key factor, with **Ted Sarandos** (Netflix) assessing potential hurdles for a higher offer.  
- **Shareholder sentiment** is split, with some wary of Netflix's deal due to cost and uncertainty, while others favor Paramount's approach.  
- **Board preferences** are unclear, as the board has not explicitly favored one deal over the other.

Tag: NVIDIA, Dell, Autodesk, NetApp, Intuit, Duolingo, Block, Meta, Core Weave

### English Summary

- **Market Trends**: S&P 500 down ~37 points (-0.5%), NASDAQ down ~270 points (-1.2%), Russell 2000 up ~0.5%. AI-related concerns weigh on equities, with mixed earnings reports and investor uncertainty.  
- **Company News**:  
  - **NVIDIA**: Strong Q2 results (beat EPS and revenue estimates), but market jitters persist due to AI demand uncertainty.

  - **NetApp**: Q3 adjusted EPS beat estimates; Q4 revenue guidance slightly below street expectations.  
  - **Intuit**: Mixed Q3 results (revenue slightly below estimates), but shares rallied in after-hours trading.  
  - **Duolingo**: Missed Q1 bookings and revenue targets; shares down 14.7% amid CFO change and performance concerns.  
  - **Block (Square)**: Announced 40% workforce cut, leading to ~15% share pop; investors speculate AI-driven restructuring.  
  - **Meta**: Shifted focus to simpler AI chips, avoiding direct competition with NVIDIA.  
  - **CoreWeave**: Fourth-quarter revenue slightly beat estimates, but shares fell in after-hours trading.
